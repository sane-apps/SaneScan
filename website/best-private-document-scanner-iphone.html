Best private document scanner for iPhone

A private scanner should do more than capture a picture of paper. It should make documents usable while keeping the workflow understandable.

Look for local document work

For sensitive records, local OCR and PDF export reduce the number of services that see your pages. You should not need an account just to scan a form.

Prefer system surfaces

Apple's document scanner, Photos picker, and share sheet are familiar and predictable. They also make permission boundaries clearer.

Avoid unclear upload claims

If a scanner sends files to a service for OCR or storage, that may be fine for some work, but it should be obvious. For private paper, no cloud upload is the simpler baseline.

Checklist for a private scanner

  • No account required before basic scanning starts.
  • Clear camera, photo, OCR, and export permissions.
  • Local text recognition for sensitive pages.
  • PDF export through the system share sheet so you choose the destination.

FAQ

Is every iPhone scanner private? No. Some scanners upload files for OCR, storage, syncing, or processing. The important thing is whether that is clearly disclosed and optional.
